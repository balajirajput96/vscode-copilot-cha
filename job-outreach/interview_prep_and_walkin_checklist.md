## PART B - CORE IPQA / QA QUESTIONS + MODEL ANSWERS
================================================================

**1. What is IPQA and why is it important?**
In-Process Quality Assurance is real-time monitoring of manufacturing operations to
ensure each stage meets defined specifications - catching issues during production
rather than after. It assures product quality, patient safety and GMP compliance.

**2. What in-process checks do you perform during tablet compression?**
- Appearance / description
- Average weight & individual weight variation
- Hardness
- Thickness
- Friability
- Disintegration time
- Group weight & tablet dimensions
- Frequency: at start, in-between (defined intervals), and at end of batch / after
  any major event (machine stoppage, RPM change).

**3. What is line clearance? Why is it critical?**
A documented check before starting an operation to confirm the area, equipment and
documents are free of any previous product, materials or labels. It prevents
mix-ups and cross-contamination - a core GMP requirement.

**4. Acceptance limits you know (typical):**
- Weight variation: as per IP/USP (e.g., +/-5% or +/-7.5% based on tablet weight)
- Friability: generally NMT 1.0% w/w
- Hardness: as per BMR specification (product specific)
- Disintegration: uncoated tablets typically within ~15 min (product specific)
(Always say "as per BMR / pharmacopoeial specification".)

**5. Difference between QA and QC?**
QA is process-oriented and preventive (systems, SOPs, documentation, compliance);
QC is product-oriented and testing-based (sampling and analysis of materials/products).

**6. What is BMR and BPR? Your role in review?**
BMR = Batch Manufacturing Record, BPR = Batch Packaging Record. I verify entries for
completeness, correctness, in-process data, reconciliation, yield, signatures and
that any deviation is documented and closed before batch release.

**7. What is a deviation? How do you handle it?**
Any departure from approved procedure/specification. Steps: identify & document ->
immediate action -> classify (minor/major/critical) -> root-cause analysis
(5-Why, Fishbone) -> CAPA -> impact assessment -> closure with QA approval.

**8. Difference between Correction, Corrective Action, Preventive Action?**
Correction = immediate fix of the problem. Corrective action = removes the root cause
so it doesn't recur. Preventive action = stops a potential problem from occurring.

**9. What is Change Control?**
A formal system to evaluate, approve, implement and review any change to process,
equipment, document or material - with impact assessment before implementation.

**10. What is GMP / cGMP / GDP / ALCOA+?**
GMP = Good Manufacturing Practices; cGMP = current GMP. GDP = Good Documentation
Practices. ALCOA+ = data must be Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original,
Accurate (+ Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available) - the basis of data integrity.

**11. What is OOS and OOT?**
OOS = Out Of Specification (result outside acceptance limit); OOT = Out Of Trend
(within limit but deviating from expected pattern). Both require investigation.

**12. What is reconciliation and yield?**
Reconciliation = accounting for all input vs output + waste (must balance within
limits). Yield = actual output vs theoretical, expressed as %.

**13. Granulation -> compression -> coating: which IPQA points?**
- Granulation: LOD/moisture, blend uniformity, sieve analysis
- Compression: weight, hardness, thickness, friability, DT, appearance
- Coating: weight gain %, appearance, no twinning/picking/sticking

**14. How do you ensure data integrity on the shop floor?**
Real-time recording (contemporaneous), no overwriting (single line cross + initial +
date for corrections), no back-dating, controlled blank forms, and following ALCOA+.

minimal training. I'm based in Vadodara, can join immediately, and your facility's
