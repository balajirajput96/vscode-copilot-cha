## PART B - SALARY NEGOTIATION (QA/IPQA, ~2 yrs, Gujarat)
================================================================

### Know your range (do your own check on Naukri/AmbitionBox/Glassdoor before quoting)
- QA/IPQA Officer with ~2 yrs in Gujarat OSD plants is commonly in a modest band;
  exact numbers vary a lot by company (MNC vs local), shift allowance and location.
- ALWAYS verify current ranges on AmbitionBox / Glassdoor / Naukri for the specific
  company before you quote. Do not rely on a fixed number from anyone.

### How to answer "What is your salary expectation?"
1. Anchor on research, not a random number:
   "Based on my 2 years of IPQA experience in tablet manufacturing and the market for
    similar roles in Gujarat, I'm looking for a fair raise over my current CTC. May I
    ask the budgeted range for this role?"
2. If pushed for a number, give a RANGE (not a single figure) and keep the lower end
   already acceptable to you.
3. Always negotiate on TOTAL CTC and ask what's included (shift allowance, PF, bonus,
   canteen, transport, gratuity).

### Practical tips
- Never quote before you know the role's responsibilities & shift.
- A bigger brand (Sun/Zydus/Lupin) on your CV can be worth more than a small hike -
  weigh learning + brand + growth, not just salary, early in your career.
- Get the offer in writing; check designation, CTC breakup, joining date, location.
- Be polite and confident; it's a discussion, not a demand.
- If two offers, it's fine to mention you have another opportunity - factually, no
  bluffing.

### Red flags to avoid
- Any "company" asking you to PAY for a job / training / equipment = SCAM. Real pharma
  companies never charge candidates. (Sun, Zydus, Amneal etc. state this on their
  career pages.)
- Verify every job posting on the company's official career page before sharing
  documents or money. Never share Aadhaar/bank details before a genuine written offer.

You've got a solid, in-demand profile - negotiate calmly and back it with your
hands-on IPQA experience. All the best!
